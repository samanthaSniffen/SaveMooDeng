Who is Moo Deng?


Moo Deng is a female pygmy hippo born in July at the Khao Kheow Open Zoo in Chon Buri, Thailand. She became an internet sensation when her zookeeper, Atthapon Nundee, shared charming videos of her bathing, sleeping, and playing online. One video alone has over 2.6 million views. The zoo even held a Facebook poll to name her, with "Moo Deng," meaning "bouncy pork," emerging as the winner. Her playful personality, from teething on her keeper to learning to navigate her pool, has endeared her to millions.

Moo Deng’s popularity has brought much-needed awareness to the endangered status of pygmy hippos. Her fame has helped shed light on the conservation efforts needed to protect these unique creatures, making her not just an online star but also a symbol of hope for her species' survival.





What does "Pygmy" mean?


"Pygmy" refers to something that is unusually small compared to others of its kind. In the animal kingdom, this term is used to describe smaller versions of species, like the pygmy hippopotamus. Unlike the much larger common hippo, pygmy hippos are more compact but no less fascinating.
